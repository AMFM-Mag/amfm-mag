ECO-FASHION

By: Ciera Mckissick

Vancouver clothing company Adhesif is a second hand surprise. Second hand things made into first rate, first ever worn pieces of clothing. Adhesif uses vintage and recycled clothing to create completely newly imagined clothing.

Fashion trends come and go, but where do all the clothes go? Adhesif creator and self taught designer Melissa Ferreria’s motto is “waste not, want not,” so her company is striving to reduce waste through their sustainable approach and materials.

Adhesif takes things like reclaimed vintage scarves and makes them into a dresses with a kaleidoscope of patterns, or creates fresh looking hooded wool sweaters made from reclaimed wool. The pieces look finished and well put together, not completely thrown together.

In 2009 the company did a limited edition denim collection called “From Rags to Riches,” where the denim was hand dyed and hand stitched to create bold colored scarves, dresses, skirts and vests embellished with vintage and designer buttons.

Offering up items for men too, the Varsity sweaters are a must have for a guy who likes that preppy geek chic look. They aren’t drab old grandfather sweaters either, they have popping colors and patterns.

Their approach to fashion has earned Adhesif recognition and accolade. They have been featured in a number of Canadian press outlets, and have been featured in Vancouver Fashion Week. They have been recognized as one of of the best eco-clothing lines.

For more Adhesif clothing, please visit adhesifclothing.com.
