Afropunk 2014

Afropunk is a two day free music, arts and cultural festival held in Brooklyn's Commodore Barry Park. The fest began as a free concert in the park for a crowd of about 100 in 2005, and has grown immensely over time into what is one of New York's largest, most diverse and fashionable gatherings and people watching experiences alongside a slew of great music acts. Inspired by the 2003 cult classic documentary "Afro-punk," which explored black punk musicians and fans in America, the festival has evolved into a movement celebrating urban alternative culture. People come from all over,

The festival is known for featuring a range of artists from across the spectrum, from punk music, to bounce, hip-hop, jazz, R&B and indie folk and rock featuring some prominent figures of this subculture in the music industry. In the past the fest has seen the likes of Janelle Monae, Erykah Badu, Pharrell Williams, Questlove, TV on the Radio, Toro Y Moi, Santigold, and many more. This year Afropunk celebrated it's tenth year anniversary with sets over the weekend from Lianne La Havas, D'Angelo, TrashTalk, Unlocking the Truth, SZA, A Tribe Called Red, The Internet, Sharon Jones and the Dap Kings, Meshell Ndegeocello and more.
