Years later, a friend, and now business partner Shawn Kahan approached him and suggested that they start a line together, and thus Cloud Nine was born.

The first t-shirt design they made together, Hella Trees, was simple and to the point, a play on the city they lived in, Sacramento, the city of trees.

Day said people thought it was clever enough to buy, an impetus enough to boost their confidence to continue forward with their clothing line.

Now they have taken their tree concept in a new direction that is rooted in the community with their new “Tee for a Tree campaign” designed to give back. For every "tee for a tree" shirt they sell, they will plant a tree in the Sacramento area.

A sense of community and giving back is very important to this collective. Last winter they teamed up with Starbucks and an elementary school for a coat drive and collected over 125 coats for kids in need. They also held an outdoor tie-dye workshop for their “Summer of Love” campaign.

“We feel that not enough young people give back to their community so we do about four things a year to help give kids a chance to give back to their community,” Day said. “The earlier the age that kids start to give back the better the world will be,” he said.

Day is helping make the world a better place with one t-shirt design at a time, and Sacramento is a better place because of Cloud Nine Clothing. Up in the clouds seems like a good place to be.
