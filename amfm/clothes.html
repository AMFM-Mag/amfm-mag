Shopping never felt better! Clothing brands that are giving back are the newest form of donation. With items like these, you can support grass root campaigns that help the world at large, while making a fashion statement!

 amfm is singling out four of the top brands out there that are making clothes, shoes, and accessories with a cause. From being added to the wardrobes of everyday people, to being talked about on Ellen, and worn by numerous celebrities, TOMS Shoes, OmniPeace, Defend New Orleans and American Apparel’s Legalize LA t-shirts are taking the world by storm—one sale at a time. 

TOMS Shoes:

Shoes are quite easy to come by in the United States. We have countless stores devoted to them, closets full of them, and electric wires decorated with them around the nation. The idea of someone not having shoes is an idea that is hard to grasp, but a grave reality for people in developing countries around the globe.

Shoes can be quite important when education and amputation is on the line. For many children and people living in developing countries, being barefoot is a way of life. Shoes protect the feet from getting wounds and cuts from unsafe roads and from contracting disease from contaminated soil, especially when walking is a primary mode of transportation. And if you’ve ever seen signs that say “No Shoes, No Service,” imagine if it read “No Shoes, No School.” 

Blake Mycoskie, founder of TOMS Shoes, decided that something needed to be done after a trip to Argentina. TOMS Shoes’ premise is simple—for every pair of shoes purchased, the company would match the pair and give it to a shoeless child. TOMS organizes “Shoe Drop Tours,” where TOMS Supporters actually travel to places like Argentina and Haiti, and hand deliver the shoes.

Since the company’s inception in Venice, California, “TOMS has given over 140,000 pairs of shoes…and plans to give over 300,000 pairs of shoes to children in need around the world in 2009,” according to their web site—and there is only more to come.

TOMS has a variety of styles, colors, designs, and even vegan options for consumers to choose from. They even have boot styles, hats, t-shirts and options for babies.

 

 

 

Check out the TOMS Shoes Web site to get your hands on a pair of shoes, and lend a helping hand. 

OmniPeace

In 2025, who knows what may become of the world. We may be living in space like The Jetsons—walking on the moon. By then we may have computer chips in our minds, so texting is instantaneous, and we may just very well be able to save Africa.

Omni Peace was created in response to seeing the devastation of poverty and disease in Sub-Saharan Africa, in hopes that it could change the conditions by the year 2025. It all started with a chocolate bar to raise money and a t-shirt, and grew into something on a much larger scale. Joining forces with Sachs and Millennium Promise, OmniPeace evolved into a full on brand.

Mary Fanaro, founder of OmniPeace, gets by with a little help from her friends. Enlisting support from her celebrity friends, Courtney Cox, Jennifer Aniston, and Naomi Campbell, Fanaro was able to simultaneously launch the brand on both the east and the west coast.

The Los Angeles based company gives 25 percent of all of their profits to end extreme poverty and promote human rights, education and peace in Africa. “OmniPeace was so successful in its first year that it sold over $300,000 for Millennium Promise and buildOn,” according to their web site.

 

OmniPeace’s slogan is “Can Fashion Save Lives?” The company has definitely proven that yes indeed it can.

Check out the OmniPeace Web site to help make the 2025 goal a possibility.
