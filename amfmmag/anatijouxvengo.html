Thalia Hall and SoundCulture Present: Ana Tijoux

The French-Chilean MC, Ana Tijoux comes to Pilsen’s very own Thalia Hall this Sunday April 19. Tijoux has been known for her conscious latin hip-hop since her emergence on the music scene with the latin hip hop group Makiza as their lead female emcee. She branched off and made a name for herself releasing her first solo album “Kaos,” which saw much success, so much so that she was nominated for a Latino MTV Music Award for best new artist and best urban artist.

Her second album “1977,” named after the year she was born was her real claim to fame as she returned to her hip hop roots rapping in both Spanish and French, after Kaos, and the pop influence of her collaborations. “1977” dealt largely with some heavy themes in her life; death, creativity, and inner strife. She later went on to release her popular EP the “Elefant Mixtape, La Bala [2011] and her most recent album, 2014’s “Vengo.”

Vengo meaning ‘I come,’ begins with the title track rapped over woodwind flutes and traditional indigenous instruments. She explores power structures, and stories of oppression, which she is certainly no stranger to considering her family’s history of fleeing from Chile to France. In Vengo, she raps “I come to you like an open book/ anxious to learn the stories of our ancestors.” She describes the album as a conversation between herself and the world, tackling feminist independence and patriarchy in “Antipatriarca,” and gentrification and modernity in “No Mas.” She even includes a conversation between herself and her one year old daughter on the final track “Emilia.”

The beauty of this album isn’t just the introspection of a seasoned musician seeking answers, but it is truly a return to the roots of music. The album despite it’s hip hop nature, is completely devoid of samples, and all of the folk and jazz inspired music featured on the album, was made in studio. Tijoux is undoubtedly a pioneer in the global hip hop movement, especially for female artists. Her show should bring to light these themes and the musical journey that she hopes to share and spread with her ever relevant and beautifully mastered music.
