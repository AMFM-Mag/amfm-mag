LIFE IN THE FASHION LANE

By: Ciera Mckissick

Fashion can definitely be theatrical, which is why Cyr cites Alexander McQueen as one of favorite designers. This past February she interned with Zac Posen in the design department while working on her masters. She contributed to the Spring/Summer, Pre-Fall, and Fall 2009 collections, which were presented at Mercedes-Benz Fashion Week in New York’s Bryant Park. When asked about her internship she gushes, “I love Zac.” It was through this internship that Cyr says she “learned about the magic of fashion.”

Cyr says she learned to “believe in herself” from her experience on Project Runway Canada, where she was a one of the top three finalists to show at L’Oreal Fashion Week in Toronto. Cyr says that trying out for the show was a joke. “I didn’t take it that seriously considering it was just a reality TV show,” she said. But, she always felt that she would make it to the top three and she did.

Despite being sent home in the fifth episode, she was able to fight her way back in the sixth in one of those wildcard challenges. During the show she coined the phrase MG is Dead to describe how she felt during her experience. “Oh God…Reality TV is not serious but yet, we all felt we were going to die on that stage,” Cyr says. “And, no one besides the contestants will ever truly understand,” she finishes.

Chicago designer, Anna Hovet, was almost a contestant on Project Runway. She was chosen to audition as a semi finalist for the show, but didn’t make the final cut. Hovet, 24 caught the attention of the people behind the show because of all of the success she’s been having since she started her own company Anna Hovet Designs nine months ago. Hovet says she’s learned so much since starting her own business.

“I had no idea how to run a business when I started!” Hovet says. “Since I started my company nine months ago I’ve been asking tons of annoying questions and reading all I can about fashion business,” she says.

Before starting Anna Hovet Designs, Hovet worked with AKIRA as a stylist, where she says she learned a lot about what it takes to run a business. “I started working at AKIRA when they were a very new company,” Hovet says. “I watched and learned as they grew into a dominating force in Chicago fashion. It was great working with costumers to learn what women want while also coordinating and styling fashion shows.”

Hovet found her way to fashion by posting photos of her designs on Facebook, where she was able to gain a bit of exposure. After she had sampled her first full collection, the next step was to send out press releases, which eventually led to her story getting picked up and featured in Lucky Magazine. Since then she has been featured in numerous Chicago blogs and publications, including the cover of the Chicago Tribune, which she seemed very excited about.

Hovet moved to Chicago from North Dakota feeling that Chicago was a good place to start off as a designer given that there are a lot of “untapped resources in Chicago.” She adds that “the city is really trying to be recognized as a fashion capital so there are many programs in motion to help independent fashion designers.” Which is why she’s not planning on leaving for awhile. She loves Chicago, and Chicago loves her. She was recently named “Readers’ Choice Best Clothing Designer” in the Chicago Reader by popular vote, and she has been chosen as one of six Macy’s Fashion Incubators, which she will end in Feburary.

“It is a really wonderful program for up and coming fashion designers,” Hovet says. “Since May I have been utilizing the office space, studio, press, and advice from industry professionals as much as I can.”

Aside from that program she has just launched her Fall 2009 and Spring/Summer 2010 line, which she says was difficult because the production seasons overlapped. She is learning from her “rookie mistakes” and knows that the future collections will get “better and better.” Hovet has always been an artist.

 “I was an artist at an early age, and as I got older got into music and pop culture, I discovered fashion,” Hovet says. “Growing up in North Dakota I was always the “weird girl” who wore pink pants and sequined boots.”

 She attended the School of the Art Institute of Chicago (SAIC), what she calls a “true art school,” where she was pushed as an artist to make very complex designs. She said it definitely paid off.

Working in the Fashion Industry is hard work, but if you have a passion for what you do, the job comes easily, and the work pays off. That doesn’t mean the days get easier though. Cameron, Cyr and Hovet have all mentioned how crazy their schedules are.

“My schedule is different every day,” Hovet says. “Some days I go from finances, to a photo shoot, to a client, to a press event, to a fashion show—it’s always crazy. Some weeks I work 90 hrs, literally sleeping at my studio.”

All in a day’s work, when you’re living life in the fashion lane…
