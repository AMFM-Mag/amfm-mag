ECO-FASHION

By: Ciera Mckissick

 

 

 

When you see a Melissa shoe, it’s like you’re in a dream-like state of awe over the plastic works of art. For 25 years Brazilian shoe company, Melissa Plastic Dreams, has created over 50 million pairs of shoes while spreading a message of technology and renewal.

Brazilian creator Zaha Hadid comes from a background in architecture and design. She was the first woman to win the Pritzker Prize in 2004 and launched Melissa in 2007.These plastic shoes are not your old school glitter jelly shoes. These shoes aren’t even shoes according to Melissa, they’re “fashion accessories.”

The myriad of styles range from flats and sandals, to heels, pumps and wedges, boots and sneaker styles. Some are adorned with a plastic bow, or are intricately designed plastic webs. No two shoes styles are the same. Melissa can be chic, while another could be punk or dapper like an english oxford.

The Melissa shoes reflect an architectural background in stature and visual complexity. Some shoe designs look more sculptural than wearable, but Melissa molds to the woman who wears them, never lacking in comfort. Technology and renewal collided to create MELFLEX, a natural material created in the Melissa Lab that has malleability, resistance and the ability to retain print designs, or create a velvet-like fabric.

The company has collaborated with many design artists, and architects around the globe, and has launched the careers of many homegrown Brazilian artists. The company and the people they work with all share a universal pledge to protect natural resources and engage in sustainable development. Plastic can be easily broken down and recycled, which is why Melissa prides itself in being nearly a “zero-waste” company.

“Today, harmony within business, society, and the environment, is the most ethical way to respect consumers and the market,” the company said on their online profile. And the consumers and the market respect Melissa Plastic Dreams for their effort and innovation.

For more shoe dreams, please visit MelissaPlasticDreams.com.
