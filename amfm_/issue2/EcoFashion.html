ECO-FASHION

By: Ciera Mckissick

 

 

 

 

Brooklyn bred WrecordsByMonkey is leaving a slight carbon footprint behind in the world of design. Not because they aren’t good enough to leave a substantial imprint, but because their recycled record designs are biodegradable to fashion. Instead of getting wrecked in landfills, over 100,000 records are transformed into accessories like bracelets, necklaces, and earrings, and even larger items like mirrors and chairs.

From what started out in a college dorm hallway, has become a well known fixture in the design world because the company combines a love for the environment, music, arts and fashion when making their pieces. As they say on their website, “Each piece was inspired by the idea of relating to music lovers of every generation and all walks of life. Records are the symbol of music, which is the universal language.”

Each collection reflects a completely different vibe, but share the same coolness. The Heritage Collection presents the more distinguished side of WBM, featuring victorian-style pendants and cameo silhouettes and charms layered with recycled records and wood.

The Off the Train Collection has an edgy and city feel to it with it’s vibrant New York cityscapes painted on bracelets with their water-based ink and paint, and word homage to Brooklyn. The Native American Collection represents our country’s roots, and is tribal stamped by WBM with its depiction of war paint and totem poles and native artisan patterns.

Then there are the classic designs like the buffalo plaid Lumberjack record cuffs, or their artist series of the cuffs featuring the abstract designs and graffiti of New York artists.

From their humble business beginnings in 2004, partners Patrick Chirico and Brian Farrell know that a little bit goes a long way. They are helping out our earth with their environmentally conscious efforts with each record they transform.
