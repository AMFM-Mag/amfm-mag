LIFE IN THE FASHION LANE DESIGNER Q&A

Meet Jessica:

amfm: what are you into right now?

Jessica Cameron: My current and consistent inspirations, in terms of design, are lines in  architecture and color harmonies in nature. 

amfm: Do you remember the first thing you designed and created?

JC: The first thing I designed entirely myself was a dress made of hair extensions. The  assignment was to use unconventional materials.  Prior to design, my passion was doing  hair for years.  I thought that the perfect way to tie together my two passions would be  to intertwine the materials… So I created a dress made of hair!

amfm: How would you describe your personal style and the pieces you design?

JC: My personal style is boho chic.  I love long skirts and dresses, flowy shirts and baggy  sweaters; paired with funky jewelry gathered from all the places I travel.  The pieces I  design, on the contrary, are very sophisticated, mature and structural with bits of  intricate detail.

amfm: What do you like better-going to fashion shows, or creating the pieces in the fashion show?

JC: I definitely prefer creating pieces for a fashion show rather than going to a fashion  show.  I have, however, been lucky enough to attend a Zac Posen and Diane Von  Furstenburg fashion show- which I cannot complain at all about!

amfm: What’s the craziest aspect of working in fashion?

JC: The craziest aspect of working in fashion is the impossible deadlines. 

Meet Marie Genevieve:

amfm: How does fashion differ in the States compared to Canada?

Marie Genevieve: Generally the same, although people are more willing to pay for  designer clothing.

amfm: Why do you love fashion? 

MG: The word “fashion” to me represents the industrialization and commercialism of the  clothing industry, I like creating at a smaller scale.  I love the idea of expressing  ourselves through clothing.

amfm: Do you remember the first thing you designed and created? 

MG: A contest of “best creative skirt”, which I won in my class in first year at fashion  school. Believe me you don’t want to see that, it was more a sculpture than a skirt.

amfm: What’s next for you? 

MG: Finishing my Masters degree at New York University, with a secret project soon to  follow, stay tuned!

amfm: You said your dream job would be to be a “well known and respected fashion  designer,” are your dreams coming true?

MG: I’m on my way… 

Meet Anna Hovet

amfm: What’s your favorite season to design for?

Anna Hovet: Summer is fun because I love the color palette and I really have to subdue  my color choices for fall. But because jackets are my favorite garments to design, fall is  always  more interesting.

amfm: Why do you love fashion?

AH: It is the most accessible and widely viewed art medium. People always express  themselves through clothing whether it is consciously or not.

amfm: Who are your favorite designers?

AH: Marc Jacobs and Vivienne Westwood

amfm: Do you remember the first thing you designed and created?

AH: The first real garment I designed and made was my Junior prom dress. It was super  cool when I made it, but I’m sure I’ll get a good laugh out of it when I’m older.

amfm: How would you describe your personal style and the pieces you design?

AH: Colorful, comfortable, edgy and stylish with a vintage vibe.
